The starting point

A Good Company That Google Was Barely Showing

Glass and Door Pro already had the experience, services and customer reputation to compete. The problem was visibility.

Its previous website was poorly built for local SEO, failed to clearly represent the quality of the company's work and attracted searches for auto glass instead of the residential and commercial glass services it actually provided.

Even when customers searched close to the company's Monroe location, Google frequently showed competitors first.

Before Viva
  • Barely visible on Google Maps
  • Ranking for the wrong types of searches
  • Receiving only 2–3 calls per week

The work

We Fixed the Foundation First

The ranking problem was not caused by one missing keyword or one incomplete profile setting. The entire local-search foundation needed to be rebuilt around the services the company actually wanted to sell.

01

Rebuilt the Website

Created a custom website that properly represented the company and organized its residential and commercial services around real customer searches.

02

Corrected the SEO Structure

Improved page targeting, service relevance, technical structure and the signals Google uses to understand what the business offers.

03

Strengthened the Google Profile

Improved the Google Business Profile so it more accurately reflected the company's services, location and target market.

04

Measured the Change

Scanned the same market before and after the work so the ranking improvement could be verified instead of assumed.

The ranking data

The Same Business. The Same Market. Sixty Days Apart.

Each scan measures where the business appears when customers search from different points throughout the market. Lower ranking numbers indicate stronger placement in Google's local results.

Before

Barely Visible Within 1.5 Miles

The business did not appear across the wider Charlotte-area scans. The scan radius had to be reduced to only 1.5 miles and centered directly on the company before meaningful ranking data appeared.

Takeaway: Customers could be located only a few miles away and still be shown competitors first.

60 Days Later
After 60 days

Ranked Number One Across the Original Radius

Using the same 1.5-mile grid, the business reached the number-one position across the measured area. The results were strong enough to justify expanding the scan.

Takeaway: The company was no longer merely appearing—it was leading the local results.

Expanded scan

Strong Rankings Across a 12-Mile Market

The scan radius was increased eightfold to test how far the improved visibility extended. The company continued ranking strongly throughout Monroe and nearby areas, with visibility beginning to push toward Charlotte.

Takeaway: The improvement held well beyond the company's immediate location.
