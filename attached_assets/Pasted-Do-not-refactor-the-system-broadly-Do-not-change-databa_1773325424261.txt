Do not refactor the system broadly. Do not change database schemas, pipeline architecture, onboarding logic, or unrelated modules.

Fix a specific workflow gap when an opportunity is marked **Closed – Lost**.

CURRENT ISSUE

When an opportunity is marked **Closed – Lost**, the system currently does the following:

- Updates the pipeline opportunity status to "lost"
- Moves the opportunity to the Closed – Lost stage
- Records a history event

However, nothing else is updated.

The linked CRM lead remains unchanged and continues to appear as an active lead (for example "New" or "In Progress"). This creates a mismatch between the Sales Pipeline and the CRM.

OBJECTIVE

When an opportunity is marked **Closed – Lost**, the linked CRM lead should automatically have its status updated so the CRM accurately reflects that the deal was lost.

IMPLEMENTATION

When an opportunity is marked **Closed – Lost**:

1. Locate the CRM lead linked to that opportunity using the existing relationship field (leadId, crmLeadId, contactId, or equivalent).

2. If a linked CRM lead exists:
   - update the CRM lead's status to **Lost**.

3. Do not delete the CRM lead. The lead should remain in the CRM for historical reference and possible future reactivation.

FOLLOW-UP TASK RULE

Follow-up tasks should **not be deleted automatically**. Existing tasks can remain unless they are explicitly managed by users. The goal is only to synchronize the CRM lead status.

DATA SAFETY RULES

- Do not delete the CRM lead
- Do not delete the opportunity
- Do not modify client/company records
- Do not refactor the pipeline structure
- Do not introduce new status systems
- Only update the CRM lead status when the opportunity is marked Closed – Lost

If no CRM lead is linked to the opportunity, do nothing.

UI RESULT

After the change:

If a deal is marked **Closed – Lost** in the pipeline:
- the opportunity shows Closed – Lost
- the CRM lead automatically updates to **Lost**
- the lead remains visible in the CRM but is clearly marked as lost

GOAL

Keep CRM and Sales Pipeline data consistent so that when a deal is lost in the pipeline, the CRM lead no longer appears as an active lead.
