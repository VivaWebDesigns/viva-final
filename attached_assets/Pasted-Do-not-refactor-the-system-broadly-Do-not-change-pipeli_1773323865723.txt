Do not refactor the system broadly. Do not change pipeline behavior, onboarding behavior, or database structure beyond what is necessary.

Fix the cleanup behavior when deleting CRM leads.

CURRENT ISSUE

The system currently uses a HARD DELETE for CRM leads. When a lead is deleted, the lead record is permanently removed from the database.

However, related records are left behind in an inconsistent state:

- Follow-up tasks have their leadId set to null, creating floating tasks
- Pipeline opportunities have their leadId set to null, creating orphaned opportunities
- Client/company records are never evaluated during deletion

This leaves inconsistent data and garbage records across the system.

OBJECTIVE

Update the CRM lead deletion logic so that clearly linked records are properly cleaned up instead of being orphaned.

The goal is that deleting a lead removes all records that depend on that lead while preserving unrelated data.

IMPLEMENTATION

Update the existing `bulkDeleteLeads` transaction so that the deletion process performs the following steps in order.

1. Identify all records clearly linked to the lead using relationship fields such as:
   - leadId
   - crmLeadId
   - contactId
   or equivalent fields already used in the system.

2. Clean up linked records as follows:

CRM Lead Notes
- Continue hard deleting these records when the lead is deleted.

CRM Lead Tag Associations
- Continue hard deleting the tag associations (not the tag definitions themselves).

Follow-Up Tasks
- Do NOT null out leadId.
- Hard delete follow-up tasks that are clearly tied to the deleted lead.

Pipeline Opportunities
- Do NOT null out leadId.
- Hard delete opportunities that are clearly tied to the deleted lead.

Clients / Companies
- Only delete a client/company record if it is explicitly linked to the deleted lead.
- If a company record is not clearly tied to that lead or could belong to other leads, do not delete it.

3. After all related cleanup is complete, hard delete the CRM lead record itself.

DATA SAFETY RULES

Do not delete unrelated records.

Only delete records that are clearly linked to the CRM lead being deleted through the existing relationship fields.

If there is uncertainty about whether a client/company record belongs exclusively to that lead, leave it in place.

SYSTEM RULES

Do not introduce soft deletes.

Do not add deleted_at or archived fields.

Do not change existing module architecture.

Do not refactor unrelated modules.

Reuse the existing delete transaction and extend it with proper cleanup logic.

GOAL

After this fix:

- Deleting a CRM lead removes the lead itself
- Lead notes and tag associations are removed
- Follow-up tasks linked to the lead are removed
- Pipeline opportunities linked to the lead are removed
- Client/company records are only removed if they truly belong to that lead

No floating tasks.
No orphaned opportunities.
No inconsistent records left behind.
